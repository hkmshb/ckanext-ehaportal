FAQ

The portal is a repository for data collected across countries we have operated in in Africa. This is developed to serve the data access, routine analysis and informed decision-making needs of government, private sector, donor, partners, and individuals.

The Data Portal contains a wide range of data sets and outputs generated from the same datasets generated through State, national, NGO, donor, and private sector sources.

Datasets are available in the following formats: JSON, CSV, Geopackage, Spatialite, GML and SHP.

Use the “Search” box at the top of the homepage- Enter keywords to search for specific datasets. You can also search by location.

If you find private data that you are interested in exploring or downloading, select the “Request Access” tab, provide requested details and submit. The portal administrator will review your request and make a decision to approve, decline or request additional information from you. A reply to your request will be sent to the email you have provided.

The information on the Data Portal is regularly updated as new data becomes available. but we cannot provide specific dates on when updates will occur.

Secondary use of the datasets by independent parties such as academic researchers etc. for non-commercial use is permitted and does not require a formal request. Data uploaded to the portal from external partners however, may be held privately and may require a formal request for access.

The portal values its users and is committed to safeguarding their privacy. Our privacy policy is designed to protect individuals with regard to the collection, storage and use of any data provided while using the portal. You can read the privacy policy here.

Approval to use the portal data only grants the user a limited, royalty-free, revocable, non-exclusive, non-transferable, non-sublicensable access. Users are not permitted to directly or indirectly, sublicense or distribute the data for which they have access to any third parties.

The commitment to accessibility for all is reflected on this site in our efforts to ensure all functionality and all content are accessible to all data.ehealthafrica.org users. The site is routinely tested for accessibility using technical standards checklist, in-depth testing with screen readers, policy experts, and people with disabilities.

The data.ehealthafrica.org website is being updated frequently to make it as accessible as possible. If you use assistive technology (such as a screen reader, eye tracking device, voice recognition software, etc.) and have difficulty accessing information on data.ehealthafrica.org, please contact us and provide the URL (web address) of the data you tried to access, the problem you experienced, and your contact information. A data.ehealthafrica.org team member will contact you and attempt to provide the information you’re seeking.
